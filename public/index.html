Engineering basis and limits

This page is intentionally transparent about what it does and does not automate. It is grounded in AISC 360-22 tension and connection-style limit states and ASME BTH-1 below-the-hook design context for rated-load workflows and fatigue-sensitive devices.

Implemented checks

  • Gross-section yielding and net-section rupture of the lug plate.
  • Block shear around the loaded edge using Ubs-adjusted tension area.
  • End-distance bearing and tear-out using an AISC J3.10-style pin proxy.

Still required outside this tool

  • ASME BTH-1 device classification, fatigue life, and service-class review.
  • Pin, shackle, cheek plate, out-of-plane bending, and local bearing verification.
  • Supporting member, attachment design, and constructability checks.
